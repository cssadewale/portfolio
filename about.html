Chapter 01

The Educator: Fifteen Years in Nigerian Classrooms

My career began in a secondary school classroom somewhere in Nigeria — chalk in hand, 40 students in front of me, and more questions than I had been trained to answer. I taught Mathematics, Further Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry across every level from JSS1 to SSS3. Fifteen years. Thousands of students. Countless exam seasons.

Teaching gave me something that no data science bootcamp teaches: the ability to understand how someone else is thinking. When a student gets something wrong, there is a reason — and finding that reason requires patience, observation, and a willingness to approach the same problem from a completely different angle. I use that skill every day I build something.

I also learned what the Nigerian education system is genuinely good at — and where it genuinely struggles. The struggle with exams is real. The reliance on paper. The manual marking. The guesswork. The lack of data about what students actually know. Those problems planted seeds that would eventually grow into CBT Pro — and into 11 more projects after it.

"Teaching taught me that the hardest part of any problem is rarely the solution. It is understanding the problem clearly enough to know what kind of solution is even possible."

Chapter 02

The Builder: When I Stopped Complaining and Started Coding

There was a specific moment. I was preparing students for an exam and I needed to run a Computer Based Test session. The options available were either expensive, unreliable on low-end Android phones, or required a laptop I did not have. I complained about it for a while. And then I stopped.

I built CBT Pro — a full-stack web exam platform — on my Android tablet, using nothing but HTML, CSS, Vanilla JavaScript, and Supabase. No laptop. No budget. I built it between marking sessions and lesson periods. It took time. It went through many broken versions. And then one day it worked, and I put real students on it for a real exam.

That experience changed everything I understood about what is possible. The constraint is never the obstacle. The constraint is the brief. Working with limited tools forced me to write lean code, make deliberate design decisions, and build only what actually mattered. I have never looked back.

CBT Pro today has CSV question upload, timed sessions, a built-in scientific calculator, anti-cheat tab detection, teacher dashboards with analytics, certificate generation, and WhatsApp exam sharing. All free. All running on GitHub Pages.

"The constraint is not the obstacle. The constraint is the brief. CBT Pro exists because I refused to wait for a better situation and decided to build one."

Chapter 03

The AI-Augmented Solutions Developer: A New Language for Old Problems

My formal tech journey began with the 3MTT programme in 2025 — Data Science track. That was the spark. Before 3MTT, I had the problems clearly identified. After 3MTT, my problem-solving instincts, inquisitiveness, and curiosity accelerated into real technical capability. I suddenly had the tools to address the classroom problems I had been observing for over a decade.

I enrolled in WorldQuant University's Applied Data Science Lab — one of the most rigorous free data science programmes in the world. I completed cohort programmes with Data Scientists Network (DSN), studied under the DSN × Google DeepTech_Ready track, and completed the DSN × Microsoft Elevate AI-900 programme. I earned certifications from IBM, DataCamp, and more.

Between programmes and teaching, I deployed 12 projects — ML models, NLP tools, EdTech platforms, and data analytics apps — all live, all solving real problems. But more than the number, I discovered something about how I work: I am not just a data scientist who occasionally uses web tools. I am an AI-Augmented Solutions Developer. I use AI as a force multiplier to operate confidently in any technical domain — not just data science. The thinking is the skill. AI extends the reach.

The EdTech projects are not coming next — they are already live. Student At-Risk Predictor, Student Study Plan Generator, Student Performance Tracker, CBT Question Bank Manager. All deployed. All built for the same classrooms I have been standing in for fifteen years.

"Data science taught me that if you can think through a problem clearly, you can find a path to a solution — even in unfamiliar territory."

How I Work

Work Philosophy & Values

🧩
Problem First, Always
I never start with a technology. I start with a problem. If the problem is not clear, the solution will not be useful. This is what fifteen years of teaching built into me.
🔧
Constraints Are Creative
CBT Pro was built with no laptop, no budget, and a slow Android tablet. The constraint forced better decisions. I now seek constraints deliberately.
📖
Learning Deliberately
Not just collecting certificates. Learning with direction — understanding why each skill connects to the next, and being honest about what I do not yet know.
🌍
Built for Context
Everything I build is designed for the Nigerian context first — slow connections, low-end devices, real classroom constraints — then scaled up. Not the other way round.
🔍
Explainability Matters
A model that produces results no one can understand is not useful in a classroom. I use SHAP and clear visualisations because teachers need to trust what the data says.
🤝
Teaching Authentically
Whether I am teaching a JSS3 student or writing a data science tutorial, I do not perform expertise. I share the actual thinking — including the mistakes.
